Trampoline

By Shel Silverstein

Bouncin' upon the trampoline/So high above the ground./Just as I was goin' up/I saw her-- comin' down. She had a daisy in her hair./ She wore a silken gown./But when she started goin' up./ I was comin' down. I tried to say, "Hello--nice day."/She smiled and spun around./"Come up awhile with me," yelled she./But I was goin' down. And so, as yet, we've never met/Because we've sadly found/That one is always goin' up/While one is comin' down. Back to Course Home Page
